Vitiligo

• Vitiligo is a disease presenting as white patches with or without white hairs in the involved area.
• Normal color of skin is due to presence of pigment called ‘Melanin’ produced by cells known as melanocytes. In vitiligo, there is destruction of melanocytes leading to decreased melanin & thus white patches. It is a common condition, roughly affecting 3-4% of population in India.
• Leucoderma means only white patches on skin & can be due to a variety of causes apart from vitiligo like chemicals, burns etc.

Causes of vitiligo

The exact cause of destruction of melanocytes is not known. However following factors are supposed to play a role:
1. Genetic – about 30-40% cases, someone in the family has the disease
2. Neural- disturbance in normal functioning of nerves which supply to skin may cause vitiligo
3. Autoimmune-body destroys its own melanocytes mistaking them as foreign substances
4. Self destruction of melanocytes due to absence of normal protective mechanisms
5. Increase in the levels of toxic metabolites in the body(free radicals)

Types of vitiligo

• Localized type-It is also known as “segmental vitiligo” where patches are seen in a single/adjacent segment of body
• Common type- It is also known as "Vitiligo vulgaris” in which patches are either     wide-spread or may involve distal asymmetrical areas.
It may be of different types like:
1. Universal type- almost entire body is involved
2. Lip-tip type- involves face mainly lips & tips of fingers
3. Focal type-with 1/2 lesions in single area

Triggering factors for vitiligo

• Physical/ emotional stress
• Rubber goods
• Chemical
• Soaps & detergents containing phenolic compounds

Management

It mainly consists of
• General understanding of nature of disease.
• Avoidance of triggering factors.
• Need for good general health & nutritious diet.

Medical therapy

 • Topical treatment(creams/lotions)
 1. Calineurin inhibitors-tacrolimus/pimecrolimus
2. Corticosteroids-mild- moderate potency
3. Basic fibroblast growth factor preparations
4. Placental extract preparations
5. Psoralen compounds
6. Vitamin D derivatives- like calcipotriol

• Oral treatment
 1. Oral psoralens
2. Supplementation of vitamins & minerals
3. Oral steroids in very small doses & for short periods
4. Levamisole
5. Rarely- azathioprine/ cyclophosphamide

• Phototherapy
Consisting of narrow band ultraviolet B rays either alone or in combination of oral psoralens.

• Surgical management
Done in case of medically unresponsive cases, where disease is non-progressive.
