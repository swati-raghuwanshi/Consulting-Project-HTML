Chemical Peel

1. What is chemical peeling?
 • It’s a procedure in which a chemical solution is used to remove top layers of skin, leading to a new smooth, healthier skin.

2. What Skin Conditions are Peel used for?
 • Acne
• Reduce fine lines and wrinkles
• Rough skin Texture
• Lentigines (large freckles) which appear as dark spots on the skin, they are also called liver spots, or age spots.
• Melasma
• Hyperpigmentation

3. What are the different types of peels?
• There are three types of chemical peels:
1. Superficial peel – targeting only superficial layers
2. Medium peel - deeper than superficial peels, but less than deep peels
3. Deep peel - strongest of the lot, with penetration into many layers of skin.

4. What should I do before the chemical peel?
 • It will be appropriate to have a consultation with your dermatologist, few weeks before a peel procedure. Doctor will put you on a pre-peel regimen consisting of moisturizers, mild cleansers, night creams, sunscreen & antioxidant tablets. The night creams should be stopped 2-3 days prior to peeling session.

5. How do I know which peel strength to choose?
 • At your appointment time, your skin will be assessed by an experienced dermatologist in order to determine the peel strength most suitable for you. In general, the concentration of peel is gradually increased with each treatment in order to maximize results

6. How is a chemical peel performed?
 • A licensed professional will cleanse your skin before gently applying layers of a solution to peel away the aged surface of the skin. Depending on the procedure, the solution is left on your face for a certain period of time before the product is removed. Then your skin will be wiped clean.

7. How would I feel while undergoing a peel?
 • Upon application, you will feel a slight stinging/ burning sensation which subsides within a few minutes.

8. How long does a treatment take?
 • A typical treatment should take about 15 -30 minutes, depending upon agent used .

9. What is the recovery time for a chemical peel?
 • Recovery time after a chemical peel varies from few hours to few days depending upon type of peel used.

10. What precautions I should take after a peel session?
 • Do not pick any spots or peel off any flaky skin
• Do not use any night creams/ creams for reducing pigmentation for next 2 days
• It is imperative that you use a sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30 and avoid direct sunlight for at least one month
• Patients with hypersensitivity to the sun should take extra precautions to guard against exposure immediately following the procedure, as they may be more sensitive following the peel.
• When washing your face do not scrub, use a gentle cleanser for sensitive skin.
• Apply a light moisturizer as often as needed to relieve dryness and tightness
• Do not have any other facial treatment for at least one week after the peel

11. What are the benefits of a chemical peel?
 • Peels take away dead skin cells and compel the generation of new skin, thus eliminating fine lines, wrinkles, sun spots, blotchiness, dryness acne and a host of other mild skin conditions.

12. What can I expect after a treatment?
 • Your skin will be given a youthful glow. It is not uncommon for people to notice your new texture and compliment it. Immediately following the treatment, your skin may be slightly red, but nothing drastic. Any redness will disappear before the end of the day.

13. Are there any risks with these peels?
 • Complications are rare and temporary in hands of expert dermatologists. Few to mention are
1. Persistent redness that may last for longer time.
2. Temporary colour change in the skin, especially in females taking birth control pills & persons who do not observe strict sun protection.

14. How many treatments should I have?
 • Generally a series of 6 sessions, each session at 2-3 weeks interval is recommended. After basic sessions are over, some maintenance peels, once a month for 3-6 months give more prolonged benefits.

15. How soon can I expect to see the effects?
 • Depending on the type of peel. For superficial peels, the results are seen almost immediately.
• For deeper peels, the results can be seen in 7-10 days.

16. Are there any side effects?
 • In the right hands, side effects with chemical peels are rare and mild. Most individuals are able to return to work or regular activities immediately after their mild peeling treatment.After a peel treatment, one may notice slight redness for up to 24 hours. This is a normaland expected occurrence indicating that the peel solution is doing its job. Sensitivity to sunlight may occasionally occur and may be prevented by using a broad spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher. Dry, flaky skin or light crusting may last up to 4 or 5 days after peel.

17. Can I do other cosmetic treatments with chemical peel?
 • Facial waxing or other facial treatments should wait at least 7 days after a peel to avoid excessive irritation.

18. Can I go right back to work?
 • In most cases, work or other normal activities may be resumed immediately.

19. Is a chemical peel painful?
 • No. There is a mild degree of "tingling" or a slightly itchy sensation while the peeling agent is in contact with the skin. Upon neutralizing the peel, the skin feels fresh and smooth without discomfort.

20. Can I go out into the sun after having a chemical peel?
 • You may go outside, but sunscreen must be worn for at least a few days after the peel as your skin will likely be a little more sensitive to sun exposure.

21. Will a chemical peel remove deep lines or wrinkles?
 • A glycolic acid peel will not remove deep lines. Laser resurfacing is recommended in most cases where wrinkles are advanced. However, a peel will often improve the tone, texture, smoothness and appearance of skin.

22. Can chemical peels be performed on areas other than the face?
 • Though most commonly done for face, the skin on any part of the body can be treated with a special mix of the chemicals used in mild and medium skin peels. As with facial chemical peels, body peels address the effects of sun damage, even out skin pigmentation, and improve skin texture.

23. Why choose Chemical Peels?
 • A chemical peel is an affordable, fast, relatively painless (comparable to a sunburn) treatment that makes a visible difference in the texture of the skin.

24. What happens if I did not continue treatment?
 • The results of a more youthful and supple skin will remain provided that a good and proper skin care regime is carried out.
