Acne/Pimples

• Acne (also known as ‘Pimples’) is a disease that affects the oil secreting glands of skin & structure associated with it (Sebaceous glands & pilosebaceous unit).
• The condition usually starts in adolescence and frequently resolves by the mid-twenties.
• Acne develops earlier in females. In 7–17% of individuals clinical acne persists beyond the age of 25 years.

Mechanism of acne formation
• Increased oil(sebum) secretion in skin.
• Clogging of pore (comedone formation).
• Infection by bacteria Propionibacterium acnes.
• Inflammation.

Causes of acne
The exact reason of acne is not known. However following factors are proposed to be involved:
• Hormonal: Hormonal imbalance with increase in the male hormones leads to increased oil(sebum) secretion & in turn acne formation.
• Genetic

Factors influencing acne

• Diet- Role of diet in acne is controversial. Though initially thought to be of no relevance, recent studies have shown that food with high glycemic index (e.g milk & milk products like ice creams) cause acne through increase in secretion of Insulin.
• Pre-menstrual flare- About 70% females exhibit acne flare 2-7 days prior to cycle.
• Sweating- In about 15% patients, exacerbation of acne is noticed with increased sweating.
• Occupation- Like exposure to oils in cooks cause acne.
• Drugs
• Stress- During illness, exams can cause acne.
• Greasy make-up.

Symptoms of acne

• It occurs most commonly on the face (99%) and to a lesser extent on the back (60%) and chest (15%).
• Severity varies from
1. Very mild disease- Having blackheads or whiteheads, few grain like lesions (papules).
2. Moderate disease- Multiple papules with or without pus filled lesions & bigger grain like lesions(nodules).
3. Severe disease- Multiple nodules with or without scars.

Management

• General principles of Acne management aims to:
1. Alleviate symptoms
2. Clear existing lesions.
3. Limit disease activity so preventing new lesions and scars developing.
4. Avoid negative impact on quality of life.

Topical treatment (creams & lotions)
• Antibiotics
• Benzoyl peroxide
• Retinoids
• Salicylic acid

Systemic treatment (tablets)
• Antibiotics
• Retinoid
• Zinc supplementation
